Our Story

A Legacy Born
of Love & Loss

01 The Beginning · 2005

A Father's Dream, Passed Into Loving Hands

In 2005, the patriarch of the family — a passionate lover of Ethiopian culture, cuisine, and hospitality — passed away, leaving behind a profound vision: a place where the warmth of an Ethiopian home could be shared with the world.

"He always said that food is not just nourishment — it is memory, it is love, it is who we are."

His four daughters — Helen, Senait, Tena, and Eden — carried that vision forward, each bringing her own unique gifts to honour his memory and build something extraordinary in the heart of Gondar.

02 The Grand Opening · 2011

Six Years of Preparation, One Unforgettable Opening

After six years of meticulous planning, sourcing heirloom recipes, and designing every detail of the space, The Four Sisters Restaurant opened its doors in 2011 — just 200 metres from the majestic Fasil's Castle, behind the public library of Gondar.

From the first day, guests were welcomed not merely as customers, but as family — seated on hand-crafted mesob baskets, served traditional injera with rich wots, and immersed in the living culture of highland Ethiopia.

03 Today · A Living Heritage

Where Every Meal Is a Cultural Journey

Today, The Four Sisters is celebrated as one of Ethiopia's finest cultural dining destinations — featured in international travel guides and beloved by visitors from over 60 countries. Yet it remains, at its heart, a family restaurant.

Every dish is prepared with recipes passed down through generations. Every ceremony is performed with the same reverence as it was in their father's home. The four sisters are always present — guiding, creating, and welcoming.

Meet the Family

The Four Sisters

Management

Helen

The eldest sister and visionary general manager. Helen orchestrates every detail of the guest experience with quiet authority and warmth, ensuring the restaurant's standards remain world-class.

Design

Senait

The creative soul behind the restaurant's breathtaking interior. Senait blends traditional Ethiopian artistry with contemporary elegance, crafting a space that feels both ancient and alive.

Dance

Tena

A classically trained dancer and cultural ambassador. Tena leads the nightly Eskista performances, bringing the vibrant rhythms of Amhara tradition to life for guests from around the world.

Tourism

Eden

The bridge between the restaurant and the wider world. Eden curates cultural tours, manages international partnerships, and ensures every visitor leaves with a deeper love for Gondar and Ethiopia.

PROGRAMS



Bonding & Heritage

Four Sisters' Family Day

Join us for an immersive family experience where generations gather to celebrate Ethiopian traditions. Learn traditional dances, prepare authentic meals together, and create lasting memories in our warm, welcoming space.




Traditional Craft

Learn How to Make Tej

Discover the ancient art of Tej-making, a traditional honey wine that has been crafted for centuries. Our expert instructors will guide you through the fermentation process, sharing secrets passed down through generations.




Senait's Specialty

Traditional Coffee Ceremony

Experience the sacred ritual of the Ethiopian coffee ceremony, led by Senait, our coffee master. From roasting the beans to the final pour, witness a ceremony that brings people together in harmony and conversation.




Culinary Mastery

Master the Art of Injera

Learn the delicate technique of making Injera, the spongy flatbread that is the foundation of Ethiopian cuisine. Our traditional chefs will teach you the fermentation secrets and perfect technique passed down through generations.




ATMOSPHERE




Every Guest Feels at Home

Crafted for Calm Moments

We believe in genuine hospitality, details, and a slower way to travel. We serve things simple: good service, cozy spaces, and moments that feel real. Every corner of The Four Sisters is designed to welcome you like family.
